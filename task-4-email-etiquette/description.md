## Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette Guide

### Part A – Professional Email Drafts
Two professional emails were written for this task:

**Email 1** — A student requesting an assignment submission deadline
extension from a professor. The email follows all professional email
rules including a clear subject line, formal greeting, well-structured
body, and proper sign-off.

**Email 2** — A student expressing interest in a summer internship
opportunity to an internship coordinator at a company. The email
demonstrates how to introduce yourself professionally and convey
genuine interest concisely.

Both drafts are saved in `email_drafts.pdf` in this folder.

### Part B – Social Media Do's and Don'ts
A checklist of responsible social media use for college students is
saved as `social-media-checklist.md` in this folder. It includes 5
Do's and 5 Don'ts covering professionalism, privacy, and responsible
online behaviour.

### Key Learning
The most important lesson from this task is that tone and structure
completely change how a message is received. A short, well-written
email with a proper subject line and respectful greeting is always more
effective than a long, poorly structured one.
