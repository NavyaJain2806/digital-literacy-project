## Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness: Cyberbullying

### Part A – Case Study
The cybercrime topic chosen for this task is **Cyberbullying**. The
case study covers what cyberbullying is, how it typically happens,
who is usually targeted, and what the consequences are for victims.

A key finding from the research was that most cases of cyberbullying
in college settings involve people the victim already knows — such as
classmates or batchmates — rather than strangers. It was also found
that victims rarely report cases, often fearing they will not be taken
seriously or that reporting will make things worse.

The full case study is saved as `casestudy.md` in this folder.

### Part B – Prevention Checklist
A 'Stay Safe Online' checklist with 8+ specific and actionable
prevention tips relevant to college students in India has been created.
It includes at least two tips specific to UPI and financial safety, and
references the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal and Helpline 1930.

The checklist is saved as `prevention-checklist.md` in this folder.

### Reflection
Researching cyberbullying revealed that it is far more personal and
close-to-home than commonly assumed. One habit to personally change
is to be more mindful of comments on others' posts online, and to
speak up when witnessing any form of online harassment rather than
staying silent.
